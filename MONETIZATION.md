# Monetization

The core loop stays free to play: pick a side, enter once, build a
career. Free is what fills the stands, and every part of the product
that creates repeat sessions (the career, the badge cabinet, the match
reports) costs us nearly nothing per user while producing organically
shareable artifacts; the newspaper-clipping report is a growth loop in
itself.

The first paid layer is identity and season structure. A season pass
scoped to a tournament or league unlocks cosmetic depth (kit and crest
customization for the generated avatar, report tone packs in the same
broadsheet voice, cabinet themes) plus quality-of-life such as extended
career history and stat splits. None of it touches scoring: paying
never changes the multiplier or the points, only how good your legend
looks, which keeps the competitive core credible. Sponsored moments
are the second layer: a bookmaker or broadcaster brands the entry
instant itself ("The 78th Minute, presented by X") and the resolved
report card, which is native inventory at exactly the seconds of
maximum attention.

The regulated future path, explicitly NOT in this build, is the
stake-on-yourself pool: players staking on their own entry clearing a
points threshold, with the TxLINE-derived probability at entry setting
the line and an on-chain settlement trail making every payout
auditable against the same feed the scoring used. That is a licensed
product in most jurisdictions and would ship only behind the
appropriate approvals, geofencing, and age gates; the architecture is
already event-sourced and replayable specifically so that settlement
disputes have a byte-exact answer.
